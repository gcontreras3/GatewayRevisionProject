Things to do

Visit the beach

Soak up the sun at Agrari Beach. A long sandy beach that has plenty of space for you and your family. It is usually split in half -- umbrellas and subeds on one side and sand on the other.
It offers crystal-clear water and more reasonably-priced restaurants, when compared to the rest of Mykonos. You'll then find one of the most relaxing beaches on the island.

How to get to Agrari Beach

It is just a 25 minute ride from Mykonos town. Alternatively, you can take the bus to Elia Beach, and then walk for 5 minutes over the rocks to Agrari.

For more information about taking the bus to the beaches look here: Mykonos Buses

Where to stay

The Sunrise Hotel & Suites is perfect for families or groups that want to stay in a quieter beach, this hotel stands right by the sea, with its pool almost on the sand.
Most of the rooms have a sea view, and the largest ones accommodate as many as six people.

More Details and Prices

Take a sail cruise

Spend the day on a sail cruise, Mykonos Sail Cruise to Delos & Rhenia is an all inclusive, full-day sailing trip to ancient Delos island.
You also stop in scenic Rhenia Island for a refreshing swim and snorkeling session in sparkling, sun-dappled waters. While sailing, the crew on board will prepare a BBQ for you and your family.

Cost for Activity:

Cost per Adult is $125

Cost per Child is $100

Target Age Group

Ages between 11 to 65 years old





Things to do with kids

Visit The Chora and the Museums

Just walking around the city will definetly be fun for the kids and parents because of its winding pathways, it can get crowded but never dangerous; The alleys have many colors on doors and windows, the cobblestone streets, and colorful paintings that they will enjoy.

Nautical Museum of the Aegean

The Nautical Museum of the Aegean is in the center of town. They have history on the Greek ancient times displaying coins and artifacts.

The Folklore museum of Mykonos

is located right next to the Nautical Museum, kids will be able to learn about the Greek islands past. They can see how rooms were made and what tools they used to build.

Tria Pigiada

A few buildings down you can visit the three wells that would provide water for the people and are still being used today. At times you can also see the inhabitants fill their buckets with water from the wells.
